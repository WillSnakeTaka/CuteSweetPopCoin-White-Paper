White Paper — Version 0 (Prelude)

Status: Experimental, Non-Final
Last Updated: January 4-9, 2026
Initial Design Period: January 4–9, 2026

Abstract

CuteSweetPopCoin (CSPC) is a symbolic, cultural, musical, spiritual, and philosophical token developed as part of the broader BahnMus universe. CSPC is not designed as an investment instrument, security, or financial product. Instead, it serves as a conceptual artifact, ritual marker, and narrative anchor for a developing Soul Economy that prioritizes mental wealth, cultural continuity, and individual creative universes.

This document represents Version 0 (Prelude) and exists to transparently record the origin, intent, and early symbolic events of CSPC. It is explicitly temporary and subject to revision.

1. Genesis Timeline

January 4, 2026

The CSPC logo was designed using legacy nostalgic indie artwork from the creator’s earlier creative period, establishing continuity between past and present artistic identities.

January 6, 2026

CSPC-T was successfully deployed and tested on the Sepolia Testnet to validate technical correctness and ensure no critical bugs or errors.

January 7, 2026

The finalized version of CuteSweetPopCoin (CSPC) was successfully deployed. This marks the formal on-chain existence of the token.

January 9, 2026

The Prelude Phase is permanently sealed.

2. Prelude Phase & Conceptual Transfers

The Prelude Phase consists of three conceptual nodes, recorded not as economic milestones but as symbolic acknowledgments.

Node 0 — Deployment

The act of deployment itself represents the first node: existence.

Node 1 — Conceptual Appreciation

A transfer of 154,000 CSPC to the creator’s own wallets, symbolically dedicated as appreciation toward a Data Cohort Teaching Assistant whose intellectual support and presence influenced the project’s early brainstorm.

Node 2 — Transactional Appreciation

Conceptually reserved for a Russian literature professor residing outside the United States, who was among the first two individuals with whom the project was discussed.

Deployment → Conceptual Appreciation → Transactional Appreciation
